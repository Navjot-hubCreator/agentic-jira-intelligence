# Agentic Jira Intelligence — Governance Framework

## 1. System Overview
This system is a multi-agent AI decision intelligence platform that transforms Jira tickets into structured execution decisions including prioritisation, risk scoring, and planning.

---

## 2. System Type
AI Decision System (Agentic AI)

---

## 3. Risk Classification
Key risks:
- Incorrect decision prioritisation
- Opaque multi-agent reasoning
- Autonomous decision risk
- Lack of accountability in agent outputs

---

## 4. Governance Framework Alignment
- NIST AI RMF (Govern, Map, Measure, Manage)
- AI risk-based control principles
- Model risk governance standards (SR 11-7 aligned thinking)

---

## 5. AI Lifecycle Governance

### Inception
- Defined use case: decision automation for engineering workflows
- Identified risk of automation bias

### Design
- Multi-agent architecture designed with controlled reasoning steps
- Explicit separation of analysis, estimation, and prioritisation agents

### Build
- Structured prompt constraints applied
- Agent outputs constrained to defined schema

---

## 6. Evaluation Framework
- Decision accuracy testing
- Agent consistency validation
- Scenario-based stress testing
- Business outcome validation

---

## 7. Explainability
- Full reasoning trace per ticket
- Decision breakdown per agent
- Score attribution for prioritisation logic

---

## 8. Controls

| Type | Example |
|------|--------|
| Preventive | structured prompts, agent constraints |
| Detective | decision logs, evaluation metrics |
| Corrective | human-in-loop override |

---

## 9. Monitoring
- Decision drift tracking
- Feedback loop from business users
- Periodic recalibration of scoring logic

---

## 10. Governance Outcome
Ensures all AI-driven decisions remain:
- explainable  
- auditable  
- human-overridable  
- aligned to business risk tolerance
